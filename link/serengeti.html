Endless Plains

Where the earth meets the sky in an unbroken golden horizon, the Serengeti's endless plains stretch beyond imagination. This vast sea of grass, covering nearly 15,000 square kilometers, is the stage for Africa's greatest wildlife dramas.

The plains are the beating heart of the Serengeti ecosystem - their nutrient-rich soils supporting millions of grazing animals and the predators that follow them. During the rainy season, they transform into lush green carpets that disappear into the distant haze.

More than just scenery, these plains offer unmatched wildlife visibility. With few trees obstructing views, you can spot hunting cheetahs from kilometers away or watch wildebeest herds moving like living rivers across the landscape.

Great Migration

Follow the world's greatest wildlife spectacle. The Great Migration in Serengeti National Park is one of the most spectacular wildlife events on Earth. Every year, over 1.5 million wildebeest, along with hundreds of thousands of zebras and gazelles, embark on a circular journey of about 1,200 miles in search of fresh grazing and water.

The Great Migration is the largest overland mammal migration in the world, following the rains and grass growth across the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem.

Unlike scheduled events, the migration follows nature's rhythm—unpredictable in timing and path. River crossings, calving seasons, and predator encounters depend on rainfall patterns, making each year's migration a unique spectacle.

Big Five Safari

Witness Africa's most iconic wildlife. The Big Five - lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhinoceros - represent the crown jewels of Serengeti National Park's incredible biodiversity. These magnificent creatures roam freely across the endless plains.

The Serengeti is one of Africa's best destinations to see all Big Five animals in their natural habitat. With vast savannas, riverine forests, and rocky outcrops, the park provides perfect habitats for these legendary species throughout the year.

Unlike zoos or confined areas, spotting the Big Five here is a true wilderness experience. Each sighting is unique, from lions hunting at dawn to leopards lounging in acacia trees.

Sunset Adventures

As the African sun dips below the horizon, the Serengeti transforms into a golden paradise. Our sunset adventures offer magical moments when predators become active, silhouettes stretch across the plains, and the sky erupts in fiery colors.

Sunset in the Serengeti is nature's daily spectacle - the perfect time for photography, sundowners, and witnessing the shift from diurnal to nocturnal wildlife. Special evening game drives extend your safari beyond normal park hours.

Unlike daytime viewing, sunset brings unique animal behaviors - lions beginning their nocturnal hunts, hippos emerging from rivers, and the haunting calls of hyenas marking their territories under the twilight sky.

About the Serengeti

Serengeti National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is nature's grandest stage. This vast ecosystem teems with incredible biodiversity: over 2 million ungulates, 4,000 lions, 1,000 leopards, 550 cheetahs, and nearly 500 bird species roam across its 15,000 square kilometers of pristine wilderness.

The Magnificent Big Five of Serengeti

Lion

Panthera leo

The apex predator of the Serengeti, living in prides of up to 30 individuals.

Elephant

Loxodonta africana

The largest land animal, consuming up to 300 pounds of vegetation daily.

Leopard

Panthera pardus

Solitary and elusive, often dragging prey up trees to avoid scavengers.

Buffalo

Syncerus caffer

Considered the most dangerous of the Big Five due to its unpredictable nature.

Rhino

Diceros bicornis

Critically endangered, with only about 5,000 remaining in Africa.

Lion Pride

Panthera leo

Female lions do 90% of the hunting for their pride.

Elephant Herd

Loxodonta africana

Led by a matriarch, herds consist of related females and their young.

Tree-dwelling Leopard

Panthera pardus

Can carry prey twice their weight up into trees.

Buffalo Herd

Syncerus caffer

Herds can number in the hundreds for protection against predators.

Rhino and Calf

Diceros bicornis

Calves stay with their mothers for 2-4 years.

Lion on Hunt

Panthera leo

Hunting success rate is only about 30%.

Elephant Bathing

Loxodonta africana

Can drink up to 50 gallons of water per day.

Leopard Stalking

Panthera pardus

Can run up to 58 km/h in short bursts.

Buffalo Defense

Syncerus caffer

Will actively defend members of their herd from predators.

Rhino Charge

Diceros bicornis

Can reach speeds of 55 km/h despite their size.

Lion Cubs

Panthera leo

Only about 1 in 8 cubs survive to adulthood.

Elephant Communication

Loxodonta africana

Use infrasound to communicate over long distances.

Leopard Resting

Panthera pardus

Spend up to 18 hours per day resting.

Buffalo Grazing

Syncerus caffer

Spend most of their day grazing on grasses.

Rhino Territory

Diceros bicornis

Males mark territory with dung piles.
