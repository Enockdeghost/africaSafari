Most Popular

Machame Route - 7 Days

7 Days Camping Moderate-Strenuous 85% Success Rate

The Machame Route, known as the "Whiskey Route," is our most popular choice offering spectacular scenery and excellent acclimatization. This route approaches from the southwest through lush rainforest, heath, and moorland to alpine desert before summiting from Barafu Camp.

Pros

  • Excellent acclimatization profile
  • Beautiful varied scenery
  • High success rate
  • Less crowded than Marangu

Cons

  • More physically demanding
  • Camping only (no huts)
  • Steep sections on days 3-4

Lemosho Route - 8 Days

8 Days Camping Moderate 90% Success Rate

The Lemosho Route is considered the most beautiful path up Kilimanjaro. Starting from the remote western side, it offers stunning vistas and abundant wildlife sightings in the early days. The route joins the Machame trail at Shira Plateau, providing excellent acclimatization.

Pros

  • Most scenic route
  • Excellent acclimatization
  • High success rate
  • Less crowded start

Cons

  • Longer duration required
  • More expensive
  • Remote starting point

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to climb Kilimanjaro?

The best times to climb Kilimanjaro are during the dry seasons: January to mid-March and June to October. These periods offer the clearest skies and most stable weather conditions. January-March tends to be colder with potential snow at the summit, while June-October has cooler temperatures but less precipitation. We generally recommend September as the ideal month with a good balance of weather conditions and fewer crowds.

How difficult is it to climb Kilimanjaro?

Kilimanjaro is a challenging but non-technical climb, meaning no special equipment or climbing skills are required. The difficulty comes from the altitude and sustained hiking over multiple days. We rate it as strenuous primarily due to the altitude factors. With proper preparation, pacing, and acclimatization (which our routes are designed for), most healthy individuals can successfully summit. Our guides are experts at setting the right pace and monitoring your condition throughout the climb.

What's included in your Kilimanjaro packages?

Our all-inclusive packages cover: park fees, camping/hut fees, rescue fees; professional mountain crew (guides, cooks, porters); all meals and drinking water; quality camping equipment (tents, sleeping mats, mess tent); pre- and post-climb accommodation; airport transfers; and KPAP porter support. We also include supplemental oxygen for emergency use, first aid kits, and comprehensive pre-climb briefings. The only things you need to bring are personal gear and tips for the crew.

How do you handle altitude sickness?

We take altitude sickness very seriously and have multiple safeguards in place. Our routes are designed with proper acclimatization profiles (climb high, sleep low). Guides conduct twice-daily health checks monitoring oxygen levels and symptoms. We carry portable oxygen and specialized medications for treatment. In cases where symptoms become severe, we'll descend immediately - our guides are trained in wilderness first response and we have evacuation protocols in place. With our approach, serious cases are rare but we're fully prepared to handle them.

What physical fitness level is required?

You should be in good physical condition with the ability to hike 5-7 hours per day over uneven terrain while carrying a daypack (10-15 lbs). We recommend 3-6 months of preparation including regular cardio (hiking, running, cycling) and strength training (especially legs and core). The best training is hiking with elevation gain while wearing your boots and pack. That said, technical fitness isn't as important as mental endurance - pacing and determination are key factors in summiting successfully.
