Saadani National Park

Tanzania's Only Coastal Wildlife Sanctuary

Explore the Wilderness

About Saadani

Saadani National Park is Tanzania's 13th national park, established in 2005, and the only wildlife sanctuary in East Africa that borders the Indian Ocean. Covering 1,100 kmÂ², this unique park combines marine and mainland ecosystems, offering a truly distinctive safari experience.

Located approximately 100 km northwest of Dar es Salaam, Saadani is where the beach meets the bush. This remarkable convergence allows visitors to witness wildlife against the backdrop of the ocean - a sight found nowhere else in Tanzania.

The park encompasses a preserved ecosystem including the former Saadani Game Reserve, the former Mkwaja Ranch area, the Wami River, and the Zaraninge Forest. This diversity of habitats supports a rich variety of flora and fauna.

Why Visit Saadani?

Discover the unique features that make Saadani an unforgettable destination

Beach Safari Experience

Witness unique scenes of wildlife against ocean backdrops - elephants strolling on sandy beaches, lions resting under coconut palms, and giraffes grazing near the shoreline. This combination of beach and bush is exclusive to Saadani.

River & Ocean Activities

Explore the Wami River by boat to see hippos, crocodiles, and a wealth of birdlife. Enjoy ocean activities like snorkeling, swimming, and relaxing on pristine beaches. The marine environment hosts dolphins, turtles, and diverse fish species.

Rich Biodiversity

Saadani hosts over 30 mammal species including elephants, lions, leopards, buffaloes, giraffes, and rare sable antelopes. The park is also a birdwatcher's paradise with more than 280 recorded bird species and important turtle nesting sites.

Popular Activities

Experience the diverse adventures that Saadani National Park offers

Game Drives

Morning and afternoon drives through diverse habitats to spot elephants, lions, buffaloes, giraffes, and various antelope species.

Boat Safaris

Cruise along the Wami River to see hippos, crocodiles, water birds, and other wildlife from a unique perspective.

Walking Safaris

Guided nature walks through the bush for an intimate experience with the park's flora and smaller fauna.

Beach Activities

Swimming, snorkeling, and relaxing on Saadani's pristine beaches with opportunities for dolphin watching.

Bird Watching

With over 280 bird species, Saadani offers exceptional birding opportunities throughout the year.

Turtle Watching

Seasonal viewing of turtle nesting and hatching events along the park's protected beaches.

Visitor Information

Essential information for planning your visit to Saadani National Park

Best Time to Visit

  • June-October: Dry season - best for wildlife viewing
  • November-February: Short rains - lush vegetation, birding season
  • March-May: Heavy rains - some areas inaccessible
  • January-March: Turtle nesting season
  • April-August: Turtle hatching season

Getting There

  • By Road: 4-5 hours from Dar es Salaam via Chalinze and Bagamoyo
  • By Air: Scheduled flights from Dar es Salaam, Zanzibar, and Arusha
  • By Boat: Possible from Zanzibar and other coastal locations
  • Park Gates: Main entrance at Saadani Village and southern gate at Mkwaja

Accommodation

  • Luxury Lodges: Fully inclusive beachfront properties
  • Tented Camps: Mid-range options with safari atmosphere
  • Budget Options: Bandas and campsites within the park
  • Nearby Villages: Guesthouses outside park boundaries

Important Tips

  • Malaria prophylaxis recommended
  • Pack light clothing plus warm layers for evenings
  • Bring binoculars, camera, sunscreen, and insect repellent
  • Respect wildlife - maintain safe distances
  • Follow all park regulations and guide instructions
