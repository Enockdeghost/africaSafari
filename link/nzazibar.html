Why Choose Zanzibar?

Pristine Beaches & Crystal Waters

Zanzibar boasts some of the world's most stunning beaches, such as Nungwi and Kendwa, with powder-soft white sands and turquoise waters perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Explore Stone Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with its labyrinth of narrow streets, historic forts, spice markets, and vibrant markets that reflect centuries of Swahili, Arab, European, and Asian influences.

World-Class Snorkeling & Diving

Discover vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life at Mnemba Atoll, Prison Island, and other prime spots, offering unforgettable underwater experiences for all skill levels.

Spice & Cultural Tours

Visit sprawling spice farms to see, smell, and taste spices like cloves, vanilla, cinnamon, and pepper, learning about Zanzibar’s nickname as the "Spice Island". Engage with local artisans and explore traditional crafts.

Adventure & Nature

Hike through lush Jozani Forest and spot rare red colobus monkeys, or explore the island's waterfalls, mangroves, and marine parks—immersing yourself in Zanzibar's breathtaking natural landscapes.

Wellness & Spa Retreats

Unwind with luxurious spa treatments, beachfront yoga, and holistic wellness programs in top resorts, designed to rejuvenate body and mind amidst tranquil surroundings.

Discover the Wonders of Zanzibar

Zanzibar, often called the “Spice Island,” is a tropical paradise that offers a blend of pristine beaches, rich history, vibrant culture, and incredible natural beauty. From the historic streets of Stone Town to the crystal-clear waters of Mnemba Atoll, Zanzibar promises an unforgettable adventure for every traveler.

Idyllic Beaches

Experience endless stretches of white sand beaches like Nungwi and Kendwa, perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports in a serene environment.

Historic Stone Town

Wander through the UNESCO-listed streets of Stone Town, where ancient Arab, European, and Swahili influences create a captivating maze of markets, mosques, and colonial architecture.

Marine Adventures

Discover vibrant coral reefs and marine life with snorkeling and diving trips to Mnemba Atoll, Prison Island, and other marine hotspots teeming with colorful fish, turtles, and dolphins.

Spice Plantations

Tour expansive spice farms where fragrant cloves, vanilla, cinnamon, and pepper are cultivated, giving Zanzibar its “Spice Island” nickname and offering aromatic experiences.

Natural Wonders

Explore lush Jozani Forest, home to rare red colobus monkeys, or visit waterfalls, mangroves, and marine parks that showcase Zanzibar’s incredible biodiversity and scenic landscapes.

Relaxation & Wellness

Unwind at luxury resorts with beachfront spas, yoga sessions, and holistic treatments that rejuvenate your body and mind in a tranquil setting.

Discover Zanzibar's Must-Visit Gems

Dive into a world of natural beauty, vibrant culture, and unforgettable experiences. Zanzibar’s top destinations promise moments that will stay with you forever—whether you seek relaxation, adventure, or cultural immersion.

Nungwi Beach

Feel the warm sun and soft sands of Nungwi, a lively beach paradise renowned for stunning sunsets, water sports, and a vibrant nightlife that energizes your evenings.

Stone Town

Step back in time through winding alleys, historic markets, and ancient architecture. Immerse yourself in Zanzibar’s rich history and vibrant culture, bursting with stories and colors.

Prison Island

Discover giant tortoises and snorkel in crystal waters. A short boat ride from Stone Town, Prison Island offers a perfect escape into nature and history combined.

Jozani Forest

Experience the lush greenery and rare red colobus monkeys. Jozani Forest is a tranquil haven that invites nature lovers for exploration and tranquility.

Dry Season (June - October, December - March)

The best time to visit Zanzibar with sunny days, low humidity, and minimal rainfall. Perfect for beach activities and water sports.

Average temperature: 28°C (82°F)

Short Rains (November)

Brief showers usually in the afternoon, with plenty of sunshine in between. Fewer tourists and lower prices.

Average temperature: 30°C (86°F)

Long Rains (April - May)

The wettest period with heavy rainfall. Some resorts close during this time, but you can find great deals.

Average temperature: 27°C (81°F)

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a visa to visit Zanzibar?

Most visitors to Zanzibar require a visa to enter Tanzania. Many nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival at the airport (cost is approximately $50-$100 depending on nationality) or apply for an e-visa in advance. Your passport should be valid for at least 6 months beyond your date of entry and have at least one blank page.

Is Zanzibar safe for tourists?

Zanzibar is generally very safe for tourists. The local people are friendly and welcoming. However, as with any travel destination, it's advisable to take normal precautions: don't leave valuables unattended on the beach, avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas, and use registered taxis. Most resorts have good security measures in place.

What currency is used in Zanzibar?

The official currency is the Tanzanian Shilling (TZS). US Dollars are widely accepted, especially in tourist areas, but you'll get better prices paying in shillings for local purchases. ATMs are available in Stone Town and larger villages. Credit cards are accepted at most hotels and larger restaurants, but it's good to carry cash for smaller establishments and markets.

What vaccinations do I need for Zanzibar?

A yellow fever vaccination certificate is required if you're arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission. Recommended vaccinations include Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and routine vaccines. Malaria prophylaxis is also recommended. Consult your doctor or a travel clinic at least 4-6 weeks before your trip for personalized advice.

What should I pack for Zanzibar?

Pack light, breathable clothing (cotton or linen), swimwear, sun protection (hat, sunglasses, high SPF sunscreen), comfortable walking shoes for Stone Town, a light sweater for cooler evenings, and a modest outfit for visiting mosques or local villages. Don't forget your camera, waterproof phone case, and any necessary medications. The dress code is generally casual but conservative outside resort areas.
