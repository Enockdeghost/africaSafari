Nestled at the base of the Great Rift Valley escarpment, Lake Manyara National Park is one of Tanzania's most diverse and scenic wildlife areas. Covering just 330 square kilometers (127 square miles), this compact park packs an incredible variety of habitats and wildlife into a relatively small area.

Park Overview

Lake Manyara National Park was established in 1960 and is named after the shallow alkaline lake that covers about two-thirds of the park during the wet season. The park is famous for its tree-climbing lions, vast elephant herds, and thousands of flamingos that flock to the lake shores.

The stunning landscape of Lake Manyara National Park

Wildlife Highlights

The park's varied habitats support an impressive array of wildlife:

Mammals

Lake Manyara is home to all of the Big Five except rhinos. The park's famous tree-climbing lions are often seen lounging in acacia trees. Large herds of elephants roam the park, along with buffalo, hippos, giraffes, zebras, wildebeests, and several species of antelope.

Birds

With over 400 species recorded, Lake Manyara is a birdwatcher's paradise. The alkaline lake attracts thousands of flamingos and other waterbirds, while the groundwater forest hosts a variety of forest species.

Flamingos feeding in the shallow waters of Lake Manyara

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (July-October) is generally the best time for wildlife viewing as animals congregate around water sources. However, the wet season (November-June) brings migratory birds and lush green landscapes, with peak flamingo numbers between December and April.

Activities

Game drives are the main activity in Lake Manyara, with both morning and afternoon options available. The park also offers:

  • Canopy walkway through the treetops
  • Cultural tours to nearby Maasai villages
  • Night game drives (in adjacent areas)
  • Birdwatching tours

Getting There

Lake Manyara is conveniently located about 126km (78 miles) west of Arusha, making it easily accessible as part of a northern Tanzania safari circuit. The drive takes about 1.5-2 hours from Arusha or 2.5-3 hours from Serengeti National Park.

Whether you're visiting for a day trip or as part of a longer safari, Lake Manyara National Park offers a wonderful introduction to Tanzania's incredible wildlife and landscapes.
