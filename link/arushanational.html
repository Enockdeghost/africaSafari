Arusha National Park

Discover the hidden gem at the foot of Mount Meru

Welcome to Arusha National Park

Nestled between the towering peaks of Mount Meru and the rolling hills of northern Tanzania, Arusha National Park is a compact gem with remarkable diversity. Despite being one of Tanzania's smaller parks (137 sq km), it offers breathtaking landscapes ranging from open grasslands to alpine moorland, and from rainforest to crater lakes.

The park is famous for its stunning scenery, diverse wildlife, and the opportunity to see both giraffes and buffalo against the backdrop of Mount Meru. Unlike most Tanzanian national parks, Arusha allows walking safaris, offering a unique way to experience Africa's wilderness.

Why Visit Arusha National Park?

Arusha National Park offers unique experiences you won't find in Tanzania's larger parks:

  • Walking Safaris: One of few Tanzanian parks that permit walking safaris with armed rangers
  • Mount Meru: Tanzania's second highest mountain (4,566m) with spectacular climbing opportunities
  • Compact Diversity: See varied landscapes and wildlife in a small, accessible area
  • Less Crowded: Enjoy wildlife viewing without the vehicle congestion of larger parks
  • Birdwatcher's Paradise: Over 400 bird species including forest specialists
  • Perfect Add-on: Ideal 1-2 day excursion before or after Kilimanjaro climbs or northern circuit safaris

Park Highlights

Ngurdoto Crater

Often called "Little Ngorongoro," this steep-sided volcanic crater has a swampy floor inhabited by buffalo, warthogs, and baboons. The crater walls create a natural enclosure offering excellent wildlife viewing opportunities.

Momella Lakes

A series of seven shallow alkaline lakes fed by underground springs. The lakes vary in color due to different algae and support large flocks of flamingos and other waterbirds. You'll often see giraffes and buffalo grazing nearby.

Mount Meru

Tanzania's second highest peak (4,566m) offers a spectacular 3-4 day climb through diverse ecological zones. The summit provides breathtaking views of Kilimanjaro and the Rift Valley.

Wildlife of Arusha National Park

While the park lacks lions and other large predators, it boasts remarkable biodiversity:

Giraffes

Large herds of Masai giraffe

Buffalo

Big herds in crater area

Colobus Monkeys

Black-and-white forest dwellers

Flamingos

Thousands at Momella Lakes

Leopards

Rare but present in forested areas

Activities

Arusha National Park offers diverse activities for different interests:

Game Drives

Morning and afternoon drives to see wildlife with Mount Meru backdrop

Walking Safaris

Guided walks through grasslands and forest with armed ranger

Mount Meru Climb

3-4 day trek to summit with spectacular views

Birdwatching

Over 400 species including many forest specialists

Best Time to Visit

The park can be visited year-round, but optimal conditions are:

  • June to October: Dry season - best wildlife viewing, clear skies
  • January to February: Dry period between rains - good conditions
  • November and April-May: Rainy seasons - lush vegetation but some roads may be difficult

Getting There

Arusha National Park is easily accessible from Arusha town:

  • Distance: Just 25km (15 miles) northeast of Arusha
  • By Road: About 45 minutes drive from Arusha
  • Gates: Main entrance at Ngongongare Gate
  • Combination: Often combined with Kilimanjaro airport transfers

Tip: The park makes an excellent day trip from Arusha or add-on before/after a Kilimanjaro climb. Many visitors combine it with a visit to nearby cultural sites or coffee plantations.
