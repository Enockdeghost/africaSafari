Welcome to Bogonyo Island

Nestled in the sparkling waters of Lake Victoria, Bogonyo Island is a hidden gem of Tanzania. This unspoiled paradise offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unforgettable experiences. As the largest island in the Ukerewe Archipelago, Bogonyo remains one of East Africa's best-kept secrets.

With its pristine beaches, diverse wildlife, and welcoming local communities, Bogonyo Island provides an authentic African experience away from the crowds. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, this island paradise has something for every traveler.

Why Visit Bogonyo Island

Discover what makes this island so special

Pristine Natural Beauty

Experience untouched beaches with golden sands and crystal-clear waters. The island's interior features lush vegetation, ancient baobab trees, and diverse ecosystems waiting to be explored.

Rich Biodiversity

Bogonyo is a haven for birdwatchers with over 200 species recorded. The surrounding waters of Lake Victoria are home to diverse fish species, including the famous Nile perch.

Authentic Cultural Experience

Engage with the warm and welcoming local communities. Learn about traditional fishing techniques, local crafts, and the unique way of life on Lake Victoria's islands.

Essential Travel Information

Everything you need to know before your visit

Getting There

  • Fly to Mwanza Airport (MWZ) from Dar es Salaam or Nairobi
  • 1.5-hour drive from Mwanza to the port of Bukoba
  • Regular boat services from Bukoba to Bogonyo Island (2-3 hours)
  • Best to coordinate transfers with your accommodation

Best Time to Visit

  • Dry Season: June to October (Best for wildlife and activities)
  • Shoulder Season: November to February (Fewer crowds)
  • Rainy Season: March to May (Lush vegetation but challenging travel)
  • Average temperatures: 25-30Â°C year-round

Travel Essentials

  • Valid passport with at least 6 months validity
  • Recommended vaccinations: Yellow Fever, Hepatitis A, Typhoid
  • Malaria prophylaxis recommended
  • Currency: Tanzanian Shilling (TZS). USD accepted in some places
  • Power: 230V, Type G sockets (UK style)
