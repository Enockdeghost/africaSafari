Materuni Waterfalls

Discover the breathtaking beauty of this natural wonder at the foothills of Kilimanjaro

Discover Materuni Waterfalls

The beautiful path leading to Materuni Waterfall is lined with coffee farms and banana plantations. The waterfall is about 70m high, and the pool at the base is great for swimming. The views of Kibo and Moshi town can be seen.

Materuni Village is the last village on the slopes of Mount Kilimanjaro before the rainforest starts. The village is inhabited by the Chagga people who are known for their coffee farming and rich cultural traditions.

Why Choose Materuni Waterfalls?

Materuni offers a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and adventure that makes it stand out from other destinations:

  • Authentic Cultural Experience: Interact with the local Chagga people and learn about their traditions
  • Perfect Day Trip: Easily accessible from Moshi or Arusha for a refreshing getaway
  • Less Crowded: Enjoy nature without the large tourist crowds of more commercial sites
  • Diverse Activities: From hiking to coffee tours to waterfall swimming
  • Stunning Scenery: Breathtaking views of Kilimanjaro and lush landscapes
  • Cool Climate: Pleasant temperatures at this elevation (about 1,500m above sea level)

About Materuni Waterfalls

The Materuni Waterfalls are located in the Materuni Village, about 15km from Moshi town at the foothills of Mount Kilimanjaro. The waterfall cascades down from a height of about 70 meters into a refreshing pool below, surrounded by lush vegetation and dramatic rock formations.

Geology and Environment

The waterfall is fed by natural springs from Mount Kilimanjaro, with water that is remarkably clean and cold. The area around the falls is part of Kilimanjaro's ecosystem, featuring rich biodiversity including various bird species and tropical plants.

Cultural Significance

For the Chagga people, the waterfall holds cultural importance as a source of clean water and a gathering place. Local legends tell stories about the spirits that inhabit the waterfall and surrounding forest.

Activities at Materuni

Visitors to Materuni Waterfalls can enjoy a variety of activities that combine nature, culture, and adventure:

Waterfall Hike

A scenic 45-minute hike through coffee plantations with local guides explaining the flora and fauna.

Waterfall Swimming

Take a refreshing dip in the natural pool at the base of the 70m waterfall.

Coffee Tour

Learn about traditional Chagga coffee production from bean to cup, including roasting demonstration.

Cultural Experience

Visit a Chagga village, see traditional homes, and learn about local customs and history.

Detailed Activity Information

Hiking to the Waterfall: The trail is moderately challenging, taking about 45 minutes each way. The path winds through coffee farms and lush vegetation, with occasional steep sections. Guides are recommended and can be arranged in Materuni village.

Swimming: The natural pool at the base of the waterfall is safe for swimming, with water temperatures around 15-18°C (59-64°F). The best time for swimming is during the drier months (June-October and January-February) when water flow is slightly reduced.

Coffee Experience: Materuni is famous for its Arabica coffee. Visitors can participate in the entire process - from picking ripe coffee cherries to roasting beans over an open fire, followed by grinding and brewing traditional Chagga coffee.

Best Time to Visit

Materuni Waterfalls can be visited year-round, but the best conditions are during the dry seasons:

  • June to October: Clear skies, less rain, and good visibility of Kilimanjaro
  • January to February: Another dry period with pleasant temperatures
  • Rainy seasons (March-May and November): The waterfall is more powerful but trails can be muddy

Getting There

Materuni Waterfalls is accessible from Moshi town (about 15km/9mi, 30-40 minute drive). Options include:

  • Guided Tour: Most convenient option with transportation included
  • Taxi: Can be hired for the day from Moshi (negotiate price in advance)
  • Public Transport: Take a dalla-dalla (minibus) to Materuni village then walk

Tip: Combine your visit with other nearby attractions like Kikuletwa Hot Springs or a coffee plantation tour for a full day experience.
