About Lake Manyara

Discover the unique ecosystem that makes this park a photographer's paradise

Park Overview

Lake Manyara National Park covers 330 sq km, with the alkaline lake occupying two-thirds of the area during wet season. The park's diverse habitats include groundwater forests, acacia woodlands, and floodplains.

  • Location: Northern Tanzania, 126km west of Arusha
  • Best Time: Dry season (July-October) for wildlife, Nov-Feb for birds
  • Key Subjects: Tree-climbing lions, flamingos, elephants, hippos

Wildlife Highlights

Home to the famous tree-climbing lions, large elephant herds, and over 400 bird species including thousands of flamingos that create stunning pink vistas across the lake.

Photography Tips

Maximize your photographic opportunities in this unique environment:

1

Golden Hour Magic

Shoot early morning when lions descend from trees and late afternoon for flamingos against sunset reflections.

2

Panning Techniques

Use slower shutter speeds (1/125s) to capture movement of elephants against the forest backdrop.

3

Reflection Shots

Position for water-level perspectives of flamingos creating mirror images on the lake surface.

Lake Manyara National Park

Discover Tanzania's diverse wildlife paradise nestled at the base of the Great Rift Valley escarpment

Unique Wildlife

Famous for its rare tree-climbing lions, Lake Manyara hosts a remarkable concentration of wildlife in a compact area. Witness elephants, giraffes, hippos, and over 400 bird species in diverse habitats.

Birdwatcher's Paradise

The alkaline lake attracts thousands of flamingos that create stunning pink vistas along the shoreline. With over 400 recorded species, it's one of Tanzania's premier birding destinations.

Diverse Ecosystems

Experience multiple ecosystems within a small area - from groundwater forests to acacia woodlands, savannah grasslands, and the expansive alkaline lake itself.

Best Time to Visit

Plan your perfect safari experience based on seasonal highlights

Dry Season (July-October)

Ideal for wildlife viewing as animals congregate around the lake and water sources. Clear skies and minimal rainfall provide perfect safari conditions.

Highlights: Excellent big game viewing, flamingo concentrations

Green Season (November-June)

Lush landscapes with migratory bird species arriving. Fewer visitors and lower rates make this an attractive time for birdwatchers and photographers.

Highlights: Bird migration, wildflowers, baby animals

Year-Round Destination

Lake Manyara offers rewarding wildlife experiences throughout the year. The permanent water source ensures consistent animal sightings regardless of season.

Note: Heavy rains may occur April-May, but the park remains accessible
