About Mbudya Island

Mbudya Island is part of the Dar es Salaam Marine Reserve System, located just a short boat ride from the bustling city of Dar es Salaam. This protected island offers a sanctuary of natural beauty with its powdery white sand beaches, turquoise waters, and lush vegetation.

Designated as a marine reserve, Mbudya Island boasts thriving coral reefs teeming with colorful fish and marine life. The island remains largely undeveloped, preserving its natural charm and offering visitors an authentic tropical island experience.

With no permanent residents, Mbudya provides the perfect escape from city life. Visitors can enjoy fresh seafood barbecues, relax under the shade of coconut palms, and swim in some of the clearest waters in Tanzania.

Why Visit Mbudya Island

Pristine Beaches

Experience untouched white sand beaches that stretch around the entire island. With no development along the shoreline, you'll find the perfect spot to relax away from crowds.

Vibrant Coral Reefs

Snorkel in protected marine areas with stunning coral formations and diverse fish species. The clear waters offer excellent visibility for underwater exploration.

Fresh Seafood

Enjoy freshly caught seafood grilled to perfection right on the beach. Local chefs prepare lobster, prawns, fish, and other delicacies with authentic Tanzanian flavors.

Popular Activities

Snorkeling

Explore vibrant coral gardens teeming with marine life just meters from shore.

Beach Relaxation

Unwind on pristine beaches under the shade of coconut palms.

Island Exploration

Walk around the entire island in about 45 minutes, discovering hidden coves.

Bird Watching

Spot various seabird species that nest on the island's rocky outcrops.

Plan Your Visit

Getting There

Accessible by boat from Dar es Salaam. Trips depart daily from the Slipway, Msasani Peninsula. The journey takes 20-30 minutes.

Best Time to Visit

June to October (dry season) offers the best weather. November to February is also pleasant but with occasional rain showers.

What to Bring

Sunscreen, hat, swimwear, towel, snorkeling gear (can be rented), cash (no ATMs on island), and camera.

Entrance Fees

Conservation fees apply (approx. $15 for non-residents, less for residents). Includes park ranger services and conservation efforts.
